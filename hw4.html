Catherine's Jewelery


Topic

I would like to build an e-commerce website called "Catherine's Jewelry" that will sell the jewelry I make. My little sister and I create jewelry as a hobby, and we have recently decided that we are going to try to sell it. As far as the message I'd like to convey on my site, I am hoping to use the angle of the jewelry being homemade, and therefore unique, as my main selling point. Because I think I'll get my start by handing out cards while at craft fairs, I also plan to emphasize the fact that I am from Saint Louis. I will have several pages that display the various piece of jewelry I make, and another where a buyer can order the pieces they want.

Content and Scope

At the very least, my site will include 4 pages of text and pictures. The pictures displayed will be of the pieces of jewelry that I wish to try to sell, they will consist of necklaces, bracelets, and earrings. In addition to pictures of the jewelry, I may also include a picture of myself as a "meet the creator" type page. On the home page, the text will be a brief description of the product categories. On the catelog page, the text would consist of a brief description of the piece of jewelry in the picture. The Shopping Cart page would have text explaining how I've partnered with paypall. And finally, if I decide to create a "meet the creator" page, there would be a brief bio on myself.

My website will be a retail service selling homemade jewelry. My website will not serve a community beyond the one at my house.

I'm targeting people who enjoy unique jewelry, but don't want to pay designer prices. This fall, my little sister and I will be selling our jewelry at the YMCA craft fair, and we are considering creating cards with our website url on it and passing them out to anyone who buys from us.
There are hundreds of jewelry sites out there, many are professional, but there are also several sites that facilitate homemade jewelry, such as sites like Etsy.
